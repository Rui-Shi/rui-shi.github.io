About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Statistics and Data Science at Auburn University with a strong background in quantitative models, data science, and computational statistics. My research focuses on graph-based learning, causal inference, longitudinal/survival data analysis, classification, and Bayesian statistics, particularly with high-dimensional and multi-disciplinary datasets. I have a proven track record of interdisciplinary research and teaching.

Research Experience

Outlier Detection with Cluster Catch Digraphs (CCDs) (Doctoral Dissertation) | 06.2022 - 12.2024

  • Developed and implemented eight novel CCD-based outlier detection algorithms and outlyingness scores.
  • Achieved 95% mean accuracy in outlier detection, outperforming established methods like LOF and DBSCAN.

Cluster Catch Digraphs with Nearest Neighbor Distance | 06.2022 - 12.2024

  • Developed UN-CCDs, a novel clustering method using Nearest Neighbor Distance (NND) for spatial randomness tests.
  • Improved clustering performance in high-dimensional data by addressing limitations of existing CCD methods.
  • Enhanced the robustness of UN-CCDs through multiple comparison with Holm's correction, descending radius testing, and lower-tailed testing.
